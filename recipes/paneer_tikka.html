Paneer Tikka

Paneer tikka is a popular North Indian appetizer that consists of cubed paneer (a type of Indian cheese) that has been marinated in a blend of spices and yogurt, skewered and grilled until crispy and charred. The marinade typically contains spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala, along with ginger, garlic, and lemon juice, which infuse the paneer with a flavorful and tangy taste. The paneer tikka is often served with a side of mint chutney or tamarind chutney and is a popular vegetarian alternative to chicken tikka. It is commonly enjoyed at Indian restaurants or as a party snack.

    Ingredients:

  • 400g Paneer (Indian cheese), cut into cubes
  • 1/2 Yogurt
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
  • 1 tsp Cumin powder
  • 1 tsp Garam masala (a blend of spices such as cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom)
  • 1 tbsp Lemon juice
  • Salt
  • 1 Onion, cubed
  • 1 Bell peppers (red, green, yellow), cubed
  • Skewers
  • Oil (vegetable or canola oil)

    • Optional ingredients for garnishing:

    Chaat Masala (tangy spice blend made of cumin, coriander, ginger, amchoor (mango powder), and other spices)
  • Chopped Fresh Cilantro (Coriander Leaves)
  • Lemon Wedges

Note: This is not an exhaustive list and the specific ingredients and measurements can vary based on the recipe.

    Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix together yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt to taste.
  2. Add paneer cubes to the marinade and mix well, making sure all the cubes are coated evenly. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight in the fridge for best results.
  3. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  4. Thread the marinated paneer, onion, and bell pepper cubes onto skewers.
  5. Brush oil over a baking sheet and place the skewers on the sheet.
  6. Bake the skewers for 15-20 minutes, or until the paneer is crispy and charred, flipping the skewers halfway through cooking.
  7. Remove from the oven and transfer the skewers to a serving plate.
  8. Garnish with chaat masala, chopped fresh cilantro, and lemon wedges, if desired.
  9. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.

Note: Paneer tikka can also be cooked on a grill or in a pan on the stovetop. Adjust the cooking time accordingly.
